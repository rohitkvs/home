About Me

My research involves building proof-of-concept hardware testbeds focused on signal detection and processing in spatially distributed systems for beyond 5G systems, in close collaboration with industry partners. This work has direct applications in satellite swarm communications, tactical communications, and commercial distributed systems. I implemented these solutions on USRP SDRs with over-the-air data transfers. We have published several papers on these topics so far, which use different versions of USRPs, with primary focus on X310 and B210 deployments. We have a few conferences and journals underway in these topics. I am currently working on extending my testbeds towards building distributed transceiver operations for beamforming and geolocation, with an aim to build capabilities suitable for integrated sensing and communication frameworks.

In the past, I also implemented some automatic modulation classification and deep-learning-based LDPC channel coding on USRP deployments.

In addition to research, I have actively contributed to teaching. I served as a teaching assistant for the following courses at NC State University:

  • ECE 570: Computer Networks (2022–2025) with Prof. Shih-Chun Lin
  • ECE 572: Optimization and Algorithms (2022–2025) with Prof. Shih-Chun Lin
  • ECE 301: Signals and Systems (2021) with Prof. Robert Heath

Prior to joining NC State, I worked as a Junior Research Fellow at IIT Roorkee in 2019 on a smart grid project titled D-SIDES. I also served as a part-time Senior Research Fellow at NIT Rourkela in 2020, where I helped develop an Android application as part of a COVID-19 emergency response system.

Before my academic career, I worked as a Technology Manager at Atria Convergence Technologies Pvt. Ltd., one of India’s leading internet service providers.
